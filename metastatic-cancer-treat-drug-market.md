<p><p>The metastatic cancer treatment drug market encompasses various therapies, notably chemotherapy and targeted therapy drugs. Chemotherapy drugs work by indiscriminately attacking rapidly dividing cancer cells, often causing side effects due to their impact on normal cells. In contrast, targeted therapy drugs are designed to specifically interfere with molecular targets associated with cancer, leading to more precise treatment with potentially fewer side effects. Both types play crucial roles in managing metastatic cancer, offering different mechanisms to combat disease progression.</p></p>
