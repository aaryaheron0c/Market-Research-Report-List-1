<p><p>The electrical humidity sensor market consists mainly of two types: capacitive and resistive sensors. Capacitive humidity sensors measure changes in the capacitance of a hygroscopic material as moisture levels fluctuate, offering high accuracy and fast response times. In contrast, resistive humidity sensors determine moisture levels by measuring the change in electrical resistance of a hygroscopic substrate, typically providing lower accuracy but being cost-effective. Both types serve various applications, from HVAC systems to industrial processes, catering to diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
