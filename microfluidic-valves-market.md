<p><p>The microfluidic valves market comprises various types including bubble-based valves that utilize gas bubbles to control fluid flow, and capillary burst valves that rely on the breaking of surface tension to enable fluid movement. PDMS-based pneumatic valves operate using pressurized air to open or close channels, whereas electrostatic valves use electric fields for manipulation. Time-delayed valves allow fluid to pass after a set period, while electrowetting valves utilize electrical signals to adjust liquid positioning. Other innovative designs continue to emerge.</p></p>
