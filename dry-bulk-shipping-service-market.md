<p><p>Dry bulk shipping services operate primarily through two market types: time charter and voyage market. In a time charter, a shipowner leases the vessel to a charterer for a specified duration, providing operational flexibility and allowing the charterer to decide routes and cargo. In contrast, the voyage market involves a one-time contract for transporting goods from one port to another, with costs tied to the specific voyage, including fuel and port charges, making it more transactional and short-term in nature.</p></p>
